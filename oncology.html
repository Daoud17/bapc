Oncology Physiotherapy Services

Oncology physiotherapy services are designed to support patients undergoing or recovering from cancer treatment, surgery, and long-term effects of illness. These services aim to manage symptoms, restore strength, and enhance quality of life during and after treatment.

Here are some key aspects of oncology physiotherapy services:

  • Pain Management: Physiotherapists use techniques such as manual therapy, therapeutic exercise, and physical modalities including heat or cold therapy to reduce pain and improve overall comfort.
  • Maintaining Mobility and Function: Individualized exercise programs help patients regain strength, flexibility, and functional movement, enabling safer performance of daily activities.
  • Lymphedema Management: Specialized techniques such as manual lymph drainage and compression therapy assist in reducing swelling and improving lymphatic flow.
  • Fatigue Management: Gradual and structured endurance-based exercise programs help combat cancer-related fatigue and improve energy levels.

All services are tailored to the individual needs of each patient, ensuring a safe, gentle, and progressive rehabilitation process. Oncology physiotherapy plays a vital role in improving physical, emotional, and functional well-being throughout the cancer journey.

Beirut Advanced Physiotherapy Center’s oncology physiotherapy program supports individuals diagnosed with cancer and undergoing treatment. Our rehabilitation approach provides a structured treatment and exercise plan with the goal of reducing and managing treatment side effects while improving mobility, strength, and overall wellbeing.

The Program consists of:

  • One-on-one sessions during pre-op and post-op, as well as during the treatment phase
  • One-on-one sessions before, during, and after radiation/chemotherapy sessions
  • Progression to group exercise sessions throughout the survivorship phase

Is Oncology Physiotherapy Rehabilitation recommended for me?

This program is recommended for individuals who:

  • Have been diagnosed with cancer
  • Are undergoing treatment (pre-op, post-op, before, during, or after radiation/chemotherapy)
  • Are experiencing side effects of treatment
